# The Science behind MindPolis

MindPolis integrates recognized psychometric frameworks and cognitive measurements to profile users on various moral and ideological dimensions. Different assessments use different models to capture your philosophical landscape. Here is a breakdown of the criteria and scales used throughout the platform:

## 1. The Political Compass model

The most famous mapping system utilizes two intersecting dimensions. Most historical 1D spectrums (Left vs Right) are extremely imprecise. The 2D compass differentiates *economic* views from *social/authoritative* views:

- **Economic Axis (X-Axis): Left vs. Right**
  - Evaluates your perspective on how the economy should be run.
  - **Left (Negative score):** Believes the economy should be run by a cooperative collective agency (which can mean the state, but can also mean a network of communes). Tends towards wealth redistribution, stronger regulations, and collective ownership or robust unions.
  - **Right (Positive score):** Believes the economy should be left to the devices of competing individuals and organizations. Tends to favor deregulated free markets, low taxes, and privatization.

- **Authority Axis (Y-Axis): Libertarian vs. Authoritarian**
  - Evaluates how much authority or control should be exercised over people's personal and social lives.
  - **Libertarian (Negative score):** Values maximal personal freedom. Dislikes state intervention in personal matters, surveillance, or strict societal hierarchies.
  - **Authoritarian (Positive score):** Values order, tradition, and state intervention. Believes a strong state or authority structure is necessary for social stability.

When taking this assessment, the mathematical framework accumulates weighted Likert-scale responses mapped to `-1` to `+1` per dimension. You are then mapped onto a geometric grid representing these axes.

## 2. Moral Foundations Theory (MFT)

MindPolis utilizes a 6-axis variant of Jonathan Haidt’s Moral Foundations Theory to analyze the subconscious intuitions that drive human morality and thus political ideology. Instead of asking what policy you support, MFT asks what moral flavors you instinctively respond to.

The six dimensions measured are:
1. **Care / Harm**: The foundation of compassion and protection of vulnerable people. High scorers focus on empathy and avoiding cruelty.
2. **Fairness / Cheating**: Evolved from the dynamics of reciprocal altruism. High scorers focus heavily on justice, rights, and autonomy.
3. **Liberty / Oppression**: The urge to rebel against coercion and tyranny. Often felt as righteous anger toward bullies.
4. **Loyalty / Betrayal**: Tied to our tribal evolutionary history. High scorers value patriotism, self-sacrifice for the group, and strong ingroups.
5. **Authority / Subversion**: Stemming from the history of hierarchical social interactions. High scorers value obedience, respect for tradition, and legitimate authority.
6. **Sanctity / Degradation**: Originally shaped by the psychology of disgust and disease avoidance. It evolved to encompass the idea of elevating the body and soul to a state of purity, rejecting moral or physical contaminants.

## 3. The Archetype Synthesis

When an assessment finishes (such as the combined multidimensional MindPolis Core assessment), a K-means heuristic is applied against your Top 3 dominant moral dimensions to categorize you into a deterministic **Archetype**. 

Archetypes are premium identifiers (e.g., "The Progressive Universalist", "The Institutional Guardian") designed to synthesize complex 8-dimensional cognitive maps into a clean, shareable identity. It looks at the interactions of your highest-scoring values to predict your overarching worldview.

## Summary

In short, there are no "Right or Wrong" answers on MindPolis. We strictly map input to recognized statistical frameworks to draw an accurate topographic map of your cognitive landscape.
